Born and raised in Vancouver, the outdoors have played an essential role in my upbringing. I was either exploring the coast, or finding myself lost in the mountains. These experiences mean the world to me, but also these memories were shaped by the world itself. In our times of urban development and human innovation, the act of sustaining our land and resources has never been more important. I wish to be a part of this innovation.

I am currently studying landscape architecture at the University of British Columbia. I have B.Sc in Natural Resources Conservation, with a Major in Global Perspectives. My academic and professional experience has helped foster critical skills of data analysis and excersising that knowledge for relevant case studies. This portfolio is a collection of my projects which helps contextualize environmental and socioeconomic conflicts, and ways to reach a resolution.

I have experience with progams that deal with design, data analysis, and administration. I also have techincal experience with design such as woodworking for art and practicality.
